Visual recognition from images and videos

Automatic recognition of visual entities under different learning scenarios.

Our research in visual recognition from images and videos is driven by the goal of building intelligent systems that can operate reliably across diverse, evolving, and real-world visual environments. We focus on core challenges such as domain generalization, where models must perform well on unseen distributions, and open-set recognition, where new or unknown categories emerge during deployment. To address these, we explore learning paradigms that move beyond traditional closed-set assumptions, enabling models to adapt with minimal supervision and generalize to novel conditions. In video understanding, we investigate how to extract semantically rich and context-aware information by modeling temporal coherence and object interactions over time. Our research also encompasses continual and incremental learning, allowing recognition systems to evolve without catastrophic forgetting as new domains or categories are encountered. Additionally, we study cross-modal visual recognition, integrating complementary modalities such as text, LiDAR, and hyperspectral data to improve semantic understanding and robustness. These diverse directions converge toward developing adaptable, scalable, and semantically grounded models for comprehensive image and video recognition.

Design of robust deep learning models

Techniques to optimize the deep learning structure for different applications.

Our research on the design of robust deep learning models centers on developing architectures and training paradigms that can withstand distributional shifts, noisy supervision, limited data, and task heterogeneity. We aim to enhance the reliability and adaptability of deep models in real-world conditions where assumptions of clean, abundant, and stationary data often break down. This includes designing continual and incremental learning frameworks that preserve past knowledge while integrating new information, and exploring self-supervised and contrastive learning strategies to extract rich representations from unlabeled data. We also investigate model calibration, uncertainty estimation, and test-time adaptation to improve decision confidence and resilience under domain shifts. Furthermore, our work incorporates generative modeling, hypernetworks, and modular architectures to promote parameter efficiency and dynamic adaptation across tasks. These efforts collectively contribute to building deep learning systems that are not only accurate but also dependable, interpretable, and scalable across a wide range of deployment scenarios.
